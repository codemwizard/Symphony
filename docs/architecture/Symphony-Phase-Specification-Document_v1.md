The following trust boundaries and enforcement substrates are complete and agents must not modify them without explicit phase-level approval:
    • Pre-Phase 2 Closure: Proof spine, policy-as-code normalization, execution determinism, and policy decision hard-binding.

Phase Execution Map (Canonical Build Order)
Phases execute sequentially based on exit criteria, except for Phase 8A through 8E, which are parallel delivery tracks sharing the same substrate to serve different markets.

Phase 3 — Constraint and Legitimacy Engine
Constitutional Purpose: Ensures decisions are not just cryptographically sound but formally legitimate under applicable rule sets.
What this phase builds:
    • 3.1 Typed Dependency Graph: Machine-traversable dependencies for every decision/fact. 
    • 3.2 Recursive Legitimacy Engine: Traces legitimacy chains upward; rejects decisions with illegitimate ancestors. 
    • 3.3 Contradiction Detection: Blocks conflicting decisions (direct, temporal, or authority-based). 
    • 3.4 Failure Composition Engine: Provides machine-readable failure decomposition rather than opaque rejections. 
    • 3.5 Authority Scope Engine: Enforces authority-to-resource binding and authority delegation. 
    • 3.6 Regulator Override Rules: Precedence rules for regulators and fraud detection. 
    • 3.7 Conflict-of-Interest Enforcement: Role-based separation of duties (e.g., submitters cannot be verifiers). 
    • 3.8 Spatial Legality and DNSH Gates: Geospatial validation against Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) criteria and statutory restrictions. 
Exit Criteria: Decisions are entirely traceable; contradictions and authority scope violations are blocked; conflict-of-interest and DNSH constraints are actively enforced.

Phase 4 — Financial Integrity and Statutory Enforcement
Constitutional Purpose: Enforces the legally correct and statutorily compliant financial consequences of decisions.
What this phase builds:
    • 4.1 Settlement Finality Engine: Enforces irrevocable, immutable post-settlement boundaries. 
    • 4.2 BoZ FX Reference Rate Authority: Exclusive, deterministic use of Bank of Zambia's authoritative rates. 
    • 4.3 Asset-to-Settlement Hard Binding: Every carbon asset is irreversibly linked to its settlement record. 
    • 4.4 Currency Legality Gates: Rejects unauthorized currencies. 
    • 4.5 - 4.8 Statutory Deductions and Allocations: Automated OMGE auto-cancellation, Levy/SOP allocation, issuance-time deductions, and ZEMA three-way benefit sharing (50% developer, 30% community, 20% National Fund). 
    • 4.9 Statutory Kill Criteria: Automatic halts triggered by double-counting, missing authorizations, or DNSH failure. 
Exit Criteria: Settlements are immutable; BoZ rates are standard; OMGE, SOP, and benefit allocations are deterministically computed per credit; and statutory kill criteria function automatically.

Phase 5 — Adapter Refactor and Methodology Runtime
Constitutional Purpose: Shifts the platform from hardcoded methodology logic to an adapter-governed methodology runtime capable of simultaneous multi-format evidence exports.
What this phase builds:
    • 5.1 - 5.4 Contract & Input Normalization: Standardized descriptors, typed I/O contracts, and explicit dependency declarations for methodologies. 
    • 5.5 - 5.10 Adapter Execution: Isolated execution sandbox, capability manifests, determinism enforcement, and a rigorous certification sequence for adapters. 
    • 5.11 - 5.12 Disclosure Adapter Framework & Packaging: Allows one deterministic evidence chain to export multiple specific artifacts (e.g., ESRS_E1, ISSB_S2, SCOPE3_CATEGORY5, PWRM0001) simultaneously. 
Exit Criteria: Core platform is free of hardcoded logic; PWRM0001 and Scope 3 Category 5 disclosure adapters are registered; outputs are deterministic and strict boundary checks are operational.

Phase 6 — UI and Field Reality Layer
Constitutional Purpose: Establishes the UI as an operator truth boundary and field capture as an evidence admissibility infrastructure.
What this phase builds:
    • 6.1 - 6.3 Interface & Intake Boundary: Descriptor-driven UI without custom frontends, real-time field data normalization, and strict intake boundaries that timestamp, hash, and bind operator identity. 
    • 6.4 - 6.7 Operational Realities: Strict role scope enforcement, offline capture/sync, conflict-safe reconciliation (never "last-write-wins" for evidence), and explicit draft-to-admitted transition lines. 
    • 6.8 - 6.13 Specialized Workspaces: Distinct operational surfaces for different roles, real-time field audit UX, dedicated VVB (Validation and Verification Body) portals, independent Regulator Review Workspaces, and Spatial Reality Interfaces for geofence validations. 
Exit Criteria: Offline submissions process properly; VVBs and Regulators can replay and access evidence independently; interfaces enforce role bounds strictly; and draft evidence is distinctly separate from admitted evidence.

Phase 7 — Multi-Methodology Scale Proof
Constitutional Purpose: Proves Symphony can onboard a second methodology utilizing only new adapter descriptors and packages without altering the core enforcement or policy substrate.
Mandatory Scope: Must select either AFOLU/ARR (Agroforestry) or Organic Waste/Methane Avoidance before Phase 5 completion.
What this phase proves:
    • 7.1 - 7.6 Cross-Methodology Proofs: Zero platform schema changes, zero truth model changes, continuous invariant enforcement, strict adapter isolation, unified multi-methodology constraints (e.g. DNSH), and simultaneous parallel reporting. 
Exit Criteria: The second methodology passes certification; parallel operations exist without cross-contamination or core code modifications.

Phase 8 Tracks (Parallel Execution)
Phase 8A — Sovereign Authorization Layer
    • Purpose: Ensures verified evidence can legally cross borders (Article 6 authorization). 
    • Builds: Machine-readable host-country authorization request packs, LoA ingestion/recording workflows, corresponding adjustment bindings, and first-transfer proof attachments. 
    • Exit Criteria: Functional authorization packs and immutable LoA ingestions that classify credits correctly. 
Phase 8B — Registry Bridge Layer
    • Purpose: Connects verified evidence to external market registries. 
    • Builds: Verra issuance and Article 6.4 export packages, a National Registry Bridge to ZEMA, and bidirectional credit state reconciliation. 
    • Exit Criteria: Successful Verra submissions, automated ZEMA issuance notifications, and accurate state synchronizations. 
Phase 8C — Tokenization and On-Chain Portability
    • Purpose: Creates cryptographic export artifacts to allow on-chain platforms to mint tokens tied to off-chain evidence. 
    • Builds: Tokenization export adapters, on-chain cryptographic audit hashes, bidirectional linkages, and off-chain/on-chain retirement reconciliation. 
    • Exit Criteria: Exports minting artifacts correctly, passing verifications, and updating internal records upon external retirement. 
Phase 8D — Corporate Disclosure Layer
    • Purpose: Services the mandatory Scope 3 corporate demand for compliance data products. 
    • Builds: ESRS E1 and ISSB S2 data packages, an Enterprise Evidence API scoped by buyer, and automated buyer-grade disclosure generations possessing a machine-readable assurance trail. 
    • Exit Criteria: Generation of ESRS E1 disclosure packs directly from evidence and correct data routing via the scoped Enterprise API. 
Phase 8E — Climate Finance and Green Bond Layer
    • Purpose: Connects evidence to capital markets via assurance data. 
    • Builds: BoZ/SEC green bond use-of-proceeds evidence packs, impact-to-capital traceability chains, cryptographic ring-fencing proofs, and read-only Underwriter Evidence Rooms. 
    • Exit Criteria: BoZ-format loan compliance reports generate cleanly, proceeds proofs are cryptographically verifiable, and underwriters can safely review evidence. 

Critical Cross-Phase Dependencies
    • Phase 5 must precede all Phase 8 Sub-Phases: Multi-artifact outputs are required before terminal integrations can be built. 
    • Phase 3 must precede Phase 8A: Legitimacy engine functionality is required for national authorities to accept authorization requests. 
    • Phase 4 must precede Phase 8B and 8E: Statutory deductions and benefit-sharing math must calculate before registry or bond reporting can occur. 
    • Phase 6 VVB Portal must precede Phase 8A: Independent VVB checks are an Article 6 requirement. 
    • Second methodology selection must precede Phase 5 completion. 

What Agents Must Not Do
    1. Do not modify the Phase 2A trust boundary/enforcement substrate. 
    2. Do not insert hardcoded methodology logic into the platform core. 
    3. Do not generate un-timestamped or un-hashed evidence intake events. 
    4. Do not build Phase 8 systems before Phase 5 adapter frameworks are live. 
    5. Do not isolate the disclosure adapter as a separate product. 
    6. Do not grant VVB portals internal administration privileges. 
    7. Do not hardcode OMGE, SOP, or benefit-sharing calculations (they are versioned policies).
