# Prohibited Misinterpretations

**Invalid Simplifications**

1. **"MGEE is a Symphony data source":** MGEE is an external sovereign process authority. Its outputs are candidate artefacts that become evidentiary data upon compositional validation. The constitutional relationship is sovereign exchange, not upstream data feed.

2. **"MADD delivery constitutes evidentiary record creation":** MADD delivery constitutes delivery of a candidate artefact to Symphony's intake boundary. The evidentiary record is constituted by constitutional anchoring after compositional validation, not by the delivery act.

3. **"MAIN attestation is Symphony's confirmation of the underlying data":** MAIN attestation is the verifying body's independent certification under an approved methodology. Symphony records it. Symphony does not confirm, endorse, or re-certify the underlying data. These are categorically distinct acts.

4. **"ZEMA registry records are authoritative; Symphony mirrors them":** ZEMA registry records are sovereign inputs to Symphony's evidentiary system. 5. **"Regulator-to-platform provenance continuity is MGEE's obligation":** From constitutional anchoring, regulator-to-platform provenance continuity within Symphony's domain is Symphony's obligation. MGEE's maintenance of its own records is a separate obligation that does not discharge Symphony's.

6. **"ZEMA Channel A and Channel B are the same authority":** These are constitutionally distinct channels governing distinct sovereign acts. A domestic registry record does not automatically constitute Article 6 authorisation. Their collapse in any document, schema, or process is a constitutional violation.

7. **"A MAIN attestation passing Wave 4 implies it passed Wave 8":** Wave 4 and Wave 8 validations are constitutionally independent. A content-complete attestation (Wave 4 pass) may bear an invalid signing key (Wave 8 fail). Joint pass is required. Neither domain implies the other.

9. **"One regulator partition's admissibility implies another's":** Admissibility is regime-local. ZEMA domestic admissibility does not imply Article 6 admissibility.
