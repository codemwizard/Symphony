## Purpose

This document establishes the constitutional doctrine of regulator sovereignty
non-collapse. It defines the non-negotiable orthogonality of each regulatory
regime within Symphony's constitutional architecture, prohibits all forms of
regulator semantic flattening, cross-regime equivalence assumption, and universal
admissibility assertion, and establishes the affirmative constitutional obligations
that arise from the coexistence of multiple sovereign regulatory authorities across
Symphony's evidence surfaces.

Symphony operates as a sovereign trust coordination substrate within the
simultaneous jurisdictional authority of multiple regulatory regimes. These regimes
are constitutionally orthogonal. Each possesses its own admissibility standards,
its own evidence class requirements, its own replay obligations, its own veto
surfaces, and its own sovereignty posture. None is reducible to any other. None
may be treated as satisfied by satisfaction of another. None may be merged,
averaged, or harmonized without destroying the constitutional independence of each.

The non-collapse doctrine is not a design preference. It is a constitutional
requirement arising from the nature of regulator sovereignty itself. A regulatory
regime that is collapsed into another is not partially honoured — it is
constitutionally violated. This document defines what that violation looks like,
what it prohibits, and what the affirmative obligations are that prevent it.

## Constitutional Scope

This document governs:

1. The constitutional definition of regulator orthogonality and its application
   to all named regulatory regimes within Symphony's jurisdiction.
2. The prohibition of regulator semantic flattening in all forms — documentary,
   architectural, evidentiary, and operational.
3. The prohibition of cross-regime equivalence assumptions, including the
   assumption that evidence satisfying one regime's admissibility standards
   satisfies another's.
4. The prohibition of universal admissibility assumptions — the assumption that
   a single proof form, evidence artifact, or attestation class is admissible
   across all regulatory regimes without regime-specific verification.
5. The affirmative doctrine of regulator-specific admissibility, defining what
   it means to satisfy each regime's requirements on that regime's own terms.
6. The constitutional rules governing jurisdictional coexistence — how multiple
   regimes operate simultaneously across Symphony's evidence surfaces without
   subordination, merger, or conflict-resolution collapse.
7. The constitutional rules governing cross-border evidence legality — the
   conditions under which evidence produced within one regime's requirements
   is legally presented in proceedings governed by another regime.
8. The sovereign regulator partitioning doctrine — the architectural and
   constitutional obligations that keep each regime's evidence surface isolated
   from contamination by other regimes' standards.
9. The regulator veto coexistence rules — the constitutional semantics governing
   the simultaneous operation of multiple regime-specific veto authorities over
   a single Symphony evidence event.
10. The prohibited misinterpretations that, if instantiated in any architectural,
    operational, or documentary artifact, constitute a violation of this doctrine.

This document does NOT govern:

- The substantive legal content of any regulatory instrument beyond its
  constitutional mapping to Symphony's architecture.
- The internal enforcement trigger implementation for any regime-specific
  requirement.
- The procedural obligations of any regulated entity toward any regulatory body.
- The constitutional amendment procedures for this doctrine, which are governed
  by CONSTITUTIONAL_AMENDMENT_AND_EVOLUTION_DOCTRINE.md.
- The authority hierarchy ranking of this document relative to Root doctrine,
  which is governed by CONSTITUTIONAL_AUTHORITY_HIERARCHY.md.

## Part I: The Constitutional Definition of Regulator Orthogonality

### 1.1 Orthogonality Defined

Two regulatory regimes are constitutionally orthogonal when:

(a) Each regime possesses its own sovereign authority to define admissibility
    standards for evidence produced within its jurisdiction;

(b) The satisfaction of one regime's admissibility standards does not constitute,
    imply, or partially satisfy the other regime's admissibility standards;

(c) Each regime's evidence class requirements are derived from that regime's own
    legislative, regulatory, or methodological foundations and may not be
    substituted by another regime's equivalent;

(d) Each regime's replay obligations are independently binding — meaning the
    destruction or degradation of replay capability sufficient for one regime is
    a constitutional violation within that regime regardless of whether replay
    capability sufficient for other regimes is preserved;

(e) Each regime's veto authority over Symphony evidence events is exercisable
    independently and does not require, defer to, or yield to another regime's
    veto determination.

Orthogonality does not imply incompatibility. Two regimes are orthogonal when
they operate on parallel constitutional tracks that do not merge, not when they
are in conflict. Jurisdictional coexistence — the simultaneous satisfaction of
multiple orthogonal regimes — is constitutionally required and architecturally
achievable. It requires that each regime be served on its own terms. It does not
require that the regimes be harmonized, merged, or made equivalent.

### 1.2 Non-Collapse as the Constitutional Consequence of Orthogonality

Non-collapse is the constitutional obligation that flows directly from
orthogonality. Because the regimes are orthogonal, no architectural, evidentiary,
or operational act may collapse them without violating each collapsed regime's
sovereignty. Non-collapse means:

(a) Each regime's evidence surface must be maintained, structured, and served
    independently of every other regime's evidence surface.

(b) No cross-regime inference may be drawn from the satisfaction of one regime's
    requirements — not as a legal shortcut, not as an efficiency optimization,
    not as a constitutional accommodation.

(c) Every artifact that asserts admissibility must assert it within a specified
    regime. No artifact may assert universal admissibility or cross-regime
    admissibility without specifying which regime's standards the assertion
    satisfies and demonstrating satisfaction of each specified regime's
    standards independently.

(d) Symphony's architecture must be capable of producing, retaining, and
    replaying evidence in the precise form required by each regime, without
    requiring that form to be compatible with, translatable to, or derivable
    from any other regime's form.

## Part II: Regulator-Specific Admissibility

### 2.1 Constitutional Principle — Admissibility Is Regime-Local

Admissibility is not a global property of a Symphony evidence artifact. It is
a regime-local determination: an artifact is admissible within a specific regime
if and only if that artifact satisfies the specific evidentiary requirements of
that regime, evaluated on that regime's own terms.

No artifact is constitutionally admissible in the abstract. Every admissibility
determination must be qualified by the regime within which it is asserted.

### 2.2 ZEMA Admissibility (REG-ZM-ZEMA)

The Zambia Environmental Management Agency exercises statutory authority over
environmental impact attestations, emissions records, and the evidentiary basis
for environmental compliance determinations within the Republic of Zambia. ZEMA
admissibility within Symphony requires:

(a) **Methodological provenance**: Evidence must carry verifiable lineage to an
    approved or recognised environmental methodology. Symphony must preserve the
    exact methodology version, effective date, and approval authority in the
    canonical evidence payload at the time of attestation production. ZEMA cannot
    verify methodology provenance retroactively from runtime metadata; the payload
    must be self-proving at replay time.

(b) **Attestation authority binding**: The signing authority that produced the
    attestation must be resolvable to a ZEMA-recognised entity class at the time
    of signing. Key lifecycle records must be replay-survivable to support this
    resolution at any future verification point.

(c) **Cross-border transfer inadmissibility without ZEMA consent**: Attestations
    produced within ZEMA's jurisdiction are not constitutionally transferable as
    emission reduction credits to foreign regimes (including Article 6 counterparty
    systems) without explicit ZEMA corresponding adjustment authorisation recorded
    within Symphony's constitutional evidence chain.

(d) **Replay obligation**: ZEMA-domain records are subject to the full replay
    survivability obligations established in REPLAY_AND_HISTORICAL_TRUTH_PRIMACY.md.
    Degradation of replay capability for ZEMA-domain records is a ZEMA admissibility
    violation independently of its status as a violation of root replay doctrine.

ZEMA admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from Verra VCS,
Gold Standard, Article 6, or EU CBAM admissibility determinations. These are
orthogonal regimes.

### 2.3 Bank of Zambia Admissibility (REG-ZM-BOZ)

The Bank of Zambia exercises authority over financial system integrity,
payment settlement records, and the evidentiary basis for financial regulatory
compliance within the Republic of Zambia. BoZ admissibility within Symphony
requires:

(a) **Settlement finality provenance**: Records that assert settlement finality
    must be cryptographically bound to the authoritative enforcement boundary
    at the time of settlement. For Wave 8 records, this is the `asset_batches`
    enforcement surface. BoZ cannot accept settlement finality claims whose
    provenance trace passes through a non-authoritative intermediate layer.

(b) **Operational continuity**: BoZ evidentiary requirements extend to Wave 4
    operational sovereignty surfaces. The Wave 4 operational record and the Wave 8
    provenance record are independently BoZ-admissible within their respective
    evidence classes. Neither substitutes for the other.

(c) **Temporal binding**: BoZ-domain records must carry verifiable temporal
    binding that satisfies Zambian statutory evidentiary standards for financial
    records. Symphony must preserve this temporal binding in replay-survivable
    form.

(d) **Cryptographic independence**: BoZ reserves the right to independently
    verify the cryptographic integrity of records within its jurisdiction using
    Symphony's published canonical schema and key registry. This right is
    constitutionally protected under EXTERNAL_VERIFIER_INDEPENDENCE_DOCTRINE.md
    and cannot be reduced by any operational efficiency consideration.

BoZ admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from ZEMA, Verra,
Gold Standard, Article 6, or EU CBAM admissibility. BoZ operates as a national
financial authority with a distinct evidence class domain.

### 2.4 Zambia Data Protection Authority Admissibility (REG-ZM-DPA)

The Zambia Data Protection Authority exercises authority under the Data Protection
Act over the collection, processing, retention, and transfer of personal and
organisational data within Symphony's operational perimeter. DPA compliance within
Symphony requires:

(a) **Data minimisation in evidence payloads**: Canonical evidence payloads must
    not carry personal data beyond what is constitutionally required for
    admissibility within other regimes. Where personal data is required by another
    regime, its inclusion must be DPA-justified on the face of the payload.

(b) **Cross-border transfer restrictions**: Evidence payloads containing personal
    data are subject to DPA cross-border transfer restrictions. Transmission of
    such payloads to Article 6 counterparty systems, EU CBAM interfaces, Verra,
    or Gold Standard does not override DPA transfer restrictions. Each transfer
    requires independent DPA-compliant authority.

(c) **Retention limits**: DPA retention limits apply to personal data within
    Symphony's evidentiary chain independently of the replay obligations arising
    from other regimes. Where a DPA retention limit and a replay obligation
    conflict, the resolution is governed by CONSTITUTIONAL_PRIORITY_AND_CONFLICT_ARBITRATION.md.
    This document does not resolve that conflict; it declares the conflict as
    constitutionally real and non-collapsible.

(d) **Sovereign data localisation**: To the extent DPA requires data localisation
    within Zambian jurisdiction, Symphony's evidence surfaces serving ZEMA, BoZ,
    and DPA must satisfy this requirement without compromising the independence
    of evidence surfaces serving international regimes.

DPA compliance is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from satisfaction of
any other regime's requirements. It operates as an independent data sovereignty
constraint across all evidence surfaces.

### 2.5 Verra VCS Admissibility (REG-INT-VERRA)

The Verra Verified Carbon Standard is an international voluntary carbon market
standard whose methodology requirements, additionality standards, registry
procedures, and verification audit requirements constitute an independent evidence
class within Symphony. Verra admissibility requires:

(a) **VCS-specific methodology provenance**: Attestations must carry verifiable
    lineage to an approved VCS methodology version. Methodology provenance required
    for Verra admissibility is distinct from and non-substitutable by ZEMA,
    Gold Standard, or any other regime's methodology provenance.

(b) **Third-party verification binding**: Verra's admissibility model requires
    third-party verification. Symphony must preserve the verifier identity,
    verification date, and verification scope in the canonical evidence payload
    in a form sufficient for independent replay by Verra-authorised auditors.

(c) **Registry synchronisation provenance**: For credits issued through Verra's
    registry, Symphony must preserve the registry issuance event, serial number,
    and vintage in the canonical payload. Registry records and Symphony records
    are not mutually substitutable as evidence; each carries independent
    admissibility weight in Verra-governed proceedings.

(d) **Double-counting prevention provenance**: Verra's admissibility model
    requires that no credit is double-counted. Symphony must preserve the full
    transfer and retirement chain for Verra-domain records in a form sufficient
    for independent verification that each credit has been counted in exactly
    one jurisdiction at any point in time.

Verra VCS admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from
Gold Standard, ZEMA, Article 6, or EU CBAM admissibility determinations.

### 2.6 Gold Standard Admissibility (REG-INT-GS)

The Gold Standard for the Global Goals maintains an independent methodology
framework, verification protocol, and registry system that is constitutionally
distinct from Verra VCS even where both operate within the voluntary carbon
market. Gold Standard admissibility requires:

(a) **GS4GG-specific methodology and SDG co-benefit provenance**: Gold Standard
    attestations must carry verifiable lineage to a GS4GG-approved methodology,
    including the SDG co-benefit assessment basis where applicable. This is a
    Gold Standard-specific evidentiary element with no equivalent in Verra VCS,
    ZEMA, or any other regime.

(b) **Independent verification authority**: Gold Standard operates its own
    verification body approval process. Symphony must preserve the Gold Standard
    Approved Validator identity, validation period, and scope in the canonical
    evidence payload. This record is not interchangeable with Verra's third-party
    verifier record.

(c) **Impact Registry binding**: Gold Standard's Impact Registry constitutes an
    independent system of record. Symphony evidence and Gold Standard Impact
    Registry records are independently admissible and independently replay-obligated.
    Neither substitutes for the other.

Gold Standard admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from
Verra VCS, ZEMA, Article 6, or EU CBAM admissibility determinations. The fact
that Verra and Gold Standard occupy the same voluntary carbon market segment does
not create equivalence between their admissibility standards. This is a prohibited
equivalence assumption under Section 3.2 of this document.

### 2.7 EU CBAM Admissibility (REG-EU-CBAM)

The European Union Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism constitutes a supranational
regulatory instrument with distinct evidentiary requirements for the embedded
carbon content of regulated goods entering the EU customs territory. EU CBAM
admissibility within Symphony requires:

(a) **Embedded carbon quantification provenance**: Evidence must carry verifiable
    provenance for the methodology by which embedded carbon was quantified, in
    a form satisfying EU CBAM implementing regulations. This methodology provenance
    is distinct from Verra, Gold Standard, or ZEMA methodology requirements.

(b) **EU CBAM declarant authority**: The declaring entity's authority to produce
    the CBAM declaration must be traceable within Symphony's signer resolution
    chain to a recognised EU CBAM-authorised declarant class.

(c) **Cross-border admissibility constraint**: EU CBAM records produced within
    Symphony for goods exported to the EU are subject to EU law evidentiary
    standards at the point of customs admission. Symphony's canonical evidence
    artifacts must be structured to remain admissible under EU administrative
    proceedings without requiring access to Symphony's runtime.

(d) **Retention obligation**: EU CBAM implementing regulations impose specific
    retention obligations. These retention obligations are independently binding
    within Symphony's EU CBAM evidence surfaces and are not satisfied by retention
    obligations arising from Zambian statutory requirements, ZEMA, Verra, or
    Gold Standard.

(e) **Scope 1–3 boundary**: EU CBAM's scope of covered emissions (Scope 1 direct,
    and progressively expanded indirect emissions) constitutes an independent
    evidence boundary. Symphony must preserve the emissions scope classification
    for EU CBAM-domain records as a separate canonical payload element.

EU CBAM admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from Verra,
Gold Standard, ZEMA, BoZ, DPA, or Article 6 admissibility. EU CBAM is a
supranational trade-regulatory instrument with its own implementing authority,
its own verification procedures, and its own evidentiary standards.

### 2.8 Article 6 Counterparty Admissibility (REG-INT-ART6)

Paris Agreement Article 6 establishes the international framework for
sovereign-to-sovereign carbon market transactions, including Article 6.2
bilateral authorisations and Article 6.4 supervised mechanism credits. Article 6
admissibility within Symphony requires:

(a) **Corresponding adjustment authorisation provenance**: Any transfer of
    emission reductions from Zambia's national determined contribution (NDC)
    to a foreign counterparty under Article 6.2 requires a Zambian corresponding
    adjustment authorisation. Symphony must preserve this authorisation in the
    canonical evidence payload in a form sufficient for independent verification
    by the receiving counterparty state and by the UNFCCC Secretariat.

(b) **First-transfer sovereign provenance**: The first transfer event for any
    Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcome (ITMO) must carry irrevocable
    sovereign provenance binding it to the originating NDC. This provenance must
    be replay-survivable at any subsequent sovereign review — including at
    Global Stocktake intervals.

(c) **Authorisation-chain completeness**: Article 6 admissibility requires that
    the full authorisation chain from project-level activity to sovereign
    authorisation to international transfer be continuously verifiable within
    the canonical evidence record. No gap in this chain is admissible within
    Article 6 proceedings.

(d) **ZEMA–Article 6 boundary**: The corresponding adjustment authorisation that
    ZEMA must provide for Article 6 transfers is a ZEMA sovereign act with
    independent ZEMA admissibility standing. It is also an Article 6 requirement
    with independent Article 6 standing. These are two distinct and non-collapsible
    admissibility obligations that the same authorisation event must satisfy. The
    fact that one authorisation event must satisfy both requirements does not
    collapse the two regimes into one. Each regime's requirements must be
    independently satisfied within the canonical payload.

(e) **Counterparty state independence**: In Article 6.2 bilateral transactions,
    the receiving counterparty state operates as an independent sovereign verifier.
    Symphony evidence must support independent verification by the counterparty
    state without access to Zambia's runtime systems, in compliance with
    EXTERNAL_VERIFIER_INDEPENDENCE_DOCTRINE.md.

Article 6 admissibility is not satisfied by, implied by, or derivable from
Verra VCS, Gold Standard, ZEMA, BoZ, DPA, or EU CBAM admissibility. Article 6
operates as an international sovereign instrument with its own procedural
authority. Voluntary carbon market admissibility and Article 6 admissibility are
constitutionally separate even when they apply to the same underlying emission
reduction activity.

## Part III: Jurisdictional Coexistence

### 3.1 The Coexistence Obligation

Jurisdictional coexistence is a constitutional obligation, not a design aspiration.
Symphony operates as a single substrate within the simultaneous authority of all
seven named regimes. No regime's authority displaces another's. No regime's
requirements excuse non-compliance with another's. Symphony must satisfy each
regime's requirements on that regime's own terms, simultaneously, across all
evidence events that fall within each regime's domain.

Coexistence does not mean convergence. It means parallel, non-interfering
satisfaction of independent obligations. The architectural mechanism by which
coexistence is achieved — separate evidence surfaces, regime-specific payload
elements, regime-specific admissibility verifiers — is an implementation matter.
The constitutional obligation that coexistence be achieved is established here and
is non-derogable.

### 3.2 Regime Scope Boundaries

The domain boundary of each regime determines which Symphony evidence events fall
within its authority. These boundaries are defined as follows:

| Regime | Boundary Trigger | Events In-Scope | Events Out-of-Scope |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| ZEMA | Environmental activity attestation or emissions record produced within Zambian jurisdiction | All asset_batches carrying ZEMA-class attestation_scope | Financial settlement records without environmental dimension |
| BoZ | Financial transaction, settlement record, or payment system operation within Zambia | Wave 4 settlement events; Wave 8 asset_batches with financial settlement dimension | Environmental-only records with no financial settlement component |
| DPA | Processing of personal or organisational data within or originating from Zambia | All payloads carrying data-subject-identifiable elements | Records with no personal data dimension |
| Verra | VCS-methodology-backed emission reduction project | asset_batches carrying VCS methodology provenance | Activities not backed by an approved VCS methodology |
| Gold Standard | GS4GG-methodology-backed activity | asset_batches carrying GS4GG methodology provenance | Activities not backed by an approved GS4GG methodology |
| EU CBAM | Regulated goods with embedded carbon exported to EU customs territory | Embedded carbon quantification records for EU-bound goods | Domestic transactions with no EU export dimension |
| Article 6 | ITMOs or internationally transferred emission reductions from Zambia's NDC | asset_batches carrying sovereign authorisation for cross-border transfer | Domestic mitigation activities with no international transfer |

Scope boundaries are evaluated per evidence event, not per project or per
participant. A single asset_batch may fall within the scope of multiple regimes
simultaneously. When it does, all applicable regime requirements apply
independently and must be satisfied independently.

### 3.3 Regime Overlap Events

Where a single Symphony evidence event falls within the scope of multiple regimes,
the following coexistence rules apply:

(a) **Independent satisfaction**: The event must satisfy each applicable regime's
    admissibility requirements independently. Satisfaction of one regime's
    requirements does not constitute or contribute to satisfaction of another's.

(b) **Payload completeness**: The canonical evidence payload for the event must
    carry all regime-specific elements required by each applicable regime. Where
    regime-specific elements would conflict if co-located in a single payload
    field, each regime's element must occupy a distinctly identified payload
    position.

(c) **Independent replay capability**: The event must support independent replay
    for each applicable regime — meaning each regime's verification surface must
    be able to replay-verify the event without relying on the data or
    infrastructure of the other regime's verification surface.

(d) **Independent admissibility assertion**: Any assertion that the event is
    admissible must specify the regime or regimes within which admissibility is
    asserted. An unqualified admissibility assertion is constitutionally invalid
    for events that fall within multiple regime scopes.

## Part IV: Cross-Border Evidence Legality

### 4.1 The Constitutional Problem

Evidence produced within one regime's jurisdiction and presented in proceedings
governed by another regime faces a fundamental constitutional tension: the
producing regime's standards and the receiving regime's standards may be
incompatible, and satisfaction of both may require evidence structures that
neither regime independently requires.

Symphony's constitutional response to this tension is not to resolve it by
picking one regime's standards as primary. It is to preserve the original
evidence in the exact form required by the producing regime and to provide
regime-specific presentation layers that structure that evidence for reception
in the receiving regime's proceedings — without altering the original evidence.

### 4.2 The Non-Alteration Obligation

The original canonical evidence payload, once admitted to Symphony's
constitutional evidence chain, may not be altered for the purpose of cross-border
admissibility. Cross-border presentation is achieved through:

(a) **Regime-specific presentation views**: Read-only structured views of the
    original canonical payload that emphasise the elements relevant to the
    receiving regime's proceedings, without modifying the underlying payload.

(b) **Regime-specific attestation overlays**: Supplementary attestations by
    authorised parties confirming the original evidence's compliance with the
    receiving regime's standards. These overlays are independently admitted
    evidence events, not modifications of the original.

(c) **Certified translation records**: Where the receiving regime requires
    documentation in a specific form or language, the translation record is
    itself an independently admitted evidence event that references the original
    canonical payload by its immutable cryptographic identifier.

### 4.3 Specific Cross-Border Corridors

The following cross-border evidence corridors are constitutionally recognised
and are subject to the non-alteration obligation:

| Corridor | Producing Regime | Receiving Regime | Specific Constraint |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| ZEMA → Article 6 | REG-ZM-ZEMA | REG-INT-ART6 | Corresponding adjustment authorisation must satisfy ZEMA standards on ZEMA's terms AND Article 6 standards on Article 6's terms within the same canonical payload |
| ZEMA → EU CBAM | REG-ZM-ZEMA | REG-EU-CBAM | EU CBAM embedded carbon quantification requirements are distinct from ZEMA's environmental compliance requirements; neither satisfies the other |
| Verra → Article 6 | REG-INT-VERRA | REG-INT-ART6 | VCS credits used in Article 6 transfers require independent Article 6 authorisation; VCS admissibility does not constitute Article 6 admissibility |
| Gold Standard → Article 6 | REG-INT-GS | REG-INT-ART6 | GS4GG admissibility does not constitute Article 6 admissibility; same non-collapse obligation as Verra → Article 6 |
| BoZ → Article 6 | REG-ZM-BOZ | REG-INT-ART6 | Financial settlement records and ITMO transfer records are distinct evidence classes; BoZ settlement admissibility is not Article 6 transfer admissibility |

## Part V: Sovereign Regulator Partitioning

### 5.1 Architectural Partitioning Obligation

Symphony's architecture must implement sovereign regulator partitioning as a
structural property, not a documentary overlay. Partitioning means that each
regime's evidence surface — the set of canonical payload elements, admissibility
verification paths, replay surfaces, and veto authorities that serve that regime —
is architecturally isolated from contamination by other regimes' surfaces.

Partitioning does not prohibit a single canonical payload from serving multiple
regimes. It prohibits the evidence surface for one regime from depending on, being
satisfied by, or being compromised by the state of another regime's evidence surface.

### 5.2 Partition Boundaries

The following partition boundaries are constitutionally mandated:

(a) **Admissibility verification isolation**: The function or mechanism by which
    ZEMA-admissibility is verified must not share state, intermediate results,
    or authority conclusions with the function or mechanism by which BoZ, Verra,
    Gold Standard, EU CBAM, or Article 6 admissibility is verified.

(b) **Replay surface isolation**: The replay path for ZEMA-domain records and
    the replay path for Article 6-domain records are independent. A failure of
    the Article 6 replay surface does not degrade ZEMA replay capability, and
    vice versa.

(c) **Veto authority isolation**: The veto authorities of each regime are
    exercised independently. A ZEMA veto of a specific evidence event does not
    constitute a veto by any other regime. An Article 6 counterparty's refusal
    to accept an ITMO does not constitute a ZEMA admissibility failure.

(d) **Key registry partitioning**: Where a signing key serves attestations within
    a specific regime's domain, the admissibility of those attestations within
    that regime is determined by that regime's key recognition standards, not by
    the key's recognition status in any other regime.

## Part VI: Regulator Veto Coexistence

### 6.1 Veto Defined

A regulatory veto is the constitutional authority of a regime to declare a specific
Symphony evidence event inadmissible within that regime's proceedings. The
exercise of a veto by one regime over an evidence event does not affect the event's
admissibility in any other regime's proceedings.

### 6.2 Veto Independence

Veto independence is the constitutional rule that each regime's veto authority
is exercised without reference to any other regime's determination. Specifically:

(a) A ZEMA veto of an attestation (e.g., for methodology non-compliance) does not
    constitute a BoZ veto of a related financial settlement record.

(b) A Verra veto of a VCS credit (e.g., for verification deficiency) does not
    constitute an Article 6 veto of the underlying emission reduction activity,
    nor does it affect the corresponding adjustment authorisation obligation.

(c) An Article 6 counterparty's refusal to recognise an ITMO transfer does not
    retroactively affect the ZEMA environmental compliance record that underpins
    the emission reduction.

(d) An EU CBAM customs authority's rejection of embedded carbon documentation
    does not affect the Verra or Gold Standard admissibility of the underlying
    carbon quantification record.

(e) A BoZ evidentiary challenge to the financial settlement provenance of a
    transaction does not affect the ZEMA environmental attestation provenance
    of the environmental component of the same transaction.

### 6.3 Veto Coexistence in Overlap Events

Where a single evidence event falls within the scope of multiple regimes, each
regime's veto authority applies independently to that event within that regime's
proceedings. An event that is simultaneously vetoed by one regime and admitted
by another is in a constitutionally stable state. No resolution mechanism exists
or is required at the architectural level — each regime's determination is
independently authoritative within that regime's jurisdiction.

Symphony must preserve the canonical evidence payload in its original form
regardless of veto determinations within any regime, because the integrity of
the replay-survivable historical record is constitutionally independent of any
regime's admissibility determination.

## Part VII: Prohibited Misinterpretations

The following misinterpretations are constitutionally prohibited. Any artifact —
documentary, architectural, operational, or analytical — that instantiates one
of these misinterpretations violates this doctrine and must be corrected before
it is relied upon as authoritative.

### 7.1 Regulator Semantic Flattening

**Definition**: The treatment of multiple regulatory regimes as expressing
equivalent or interchangeable evidentiary requirements, such that a single
evidence artifact or admissibility determination is asserted to satisfy all
applicable regimes without demonstrating independent satisfaction of each.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Characterising Verra VCS and Gold Standard as equivalent evidence standards
  because both serve the voluntary carbon market.
- Characterising ZEMA and BoZ as satisfiable by a single compliance record
  because both are Zambian regulatory authorities.
- Asserting that an Article 6 authorisation simultaneously satisfies all other
  regimes' requirements for the same underlying emission reduction.
- Asserting that EU CBAM documentation satisfies Verra or Gold Standard
  methodology requirements because both address carbon.
- Treating DPA data minimisation compliance as satisfying BoZ evidentiary
  completeness requirements or vice versa.

### 7.2 Cross-Regime Equivalence Assumptions

**Definition**: The assumption that the meaning, scope, or effect of an
evidentiary concept in one regime is equivalent to the meaning, scope, or effect
of a nominally similar concept in another regime.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Treating "verification" as equivalent across Verra, Gold Standard, and Article 6.
- Treating "methodology" as equivalent across ZEMA, Verra, Gold Standard, and
  EU CBAM.
- Treating "authorisation" as equivalent across ZEMA, Article 6, and BoZ.
- Treating "retirement" as equivalent across Verra, Gold Standard, and
  Article 6 accounting.
- Treating "additionality" as equivalent across Verra VCS, Gold Standard,
  and Article 6 mechanisms.

### 7.3 Universal Admissibility Assumptions

**Definition**: The assertion that a single proof, evidence artifact, attestation
class, or cryptographic verification demonstrates admissibility across all
applicable regimes without regime-specific analysis.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Asserting that a cryptographically valid Symphony attestation is admissible in
  all applicable regulatory proceedings.
- Asserting that compliance with ISO 14064 or any other third standard constitutes
  admissibility within all named regimes.
- Asserting that a Gold Standard Impact Registry certificate satisfies Article 6
  counterparty admissibility requirements.
- Asserting that BoZ settlement finality implies ZEMA environmental compliance.
- Asserting that ZEMA environmental compliance implies Article 6 transfer eligibility.

### 7.4 "One Proof Satisfies All Regulators" Assumption

**Definition**: The specific and particularly dangerous form of universal
admissibility assumption that asserts that a single proof artifact — however
constructed — can be submitted to all applicable regulatory proceedings as
sufficient evidence of compliance within each.

This assumption is constitutionally prohibited in all forms, including:

- A single Symphony evidence export file asserted as admissible in ZEMA, BoZ,
  Verra, Gold Standard, EU CBAM, and Article 6 proceedings simultaneously
  without regime-specific analysis.
- A single cryptographic verification result asserted as constituting admissibility
  proof across all regimes.
- A single audit report asserted as satisfying all regimes' third-party
  verification requirements.
- An "omnibus compliance certificate" of any description.

### 7.5 Replay-Destructive Regime Reduction

**Definition**: The reduction of replay obligations to a single lowest-common-
denominator retention and verification standard asserted to satisfy all regimes,
where that standard is in fact derived from the least demanding regime.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Setting Symphony's replay retention period to the shortest regime-specific
  retention period and asserting compliance with all regimes.
- Reducing canonical payload elements to those required by the least demanding
  regime and asserting that the reduced payload supports replay for all regimes.
- Deleting regime-specific payload elements when they are no longer required
  by the most operationally active regime, without verifying that no other
  regime retains a replay-survivability interest in those elements.

### 7.6 Regulator Subordination

**Definition**: The treatment of one regime as authoritative over another —
the assumption that satisfaction of a "primary" regime's requirements constitutes
satisfaction of "secondary" regimes' requirements, or that one regime's veto
overrides another's admission.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Treating BoZ as the primary Zambian authority whose satisfaction implies ZEMA
  and DPA satisfaction.
- Treating Article 6 as the ultimate international authority whose requirements
  subsume Verra, Gold Standard, and EU CBAM requirements.
- Treating ZEMA as the environmental authority whose methodology approval implies
  Verra or Gold Standard methodology approval.
- Treating EU CBAM as a supranational authority whose requirements override
  Zambian regulatory requirements for the same evidence event.

### 7.7 Inactive Regime Implies Optional Regime

**Definition**: The interpretation that a regulatory regime with no current
active proceedings, enforcement actions, or reporting demands against Symphony
is therefore optional, dormant, or reducible in its constitutional standing.

**Prohibited instantiations include**:

- Reducing DPA compliance infrastructure because no DPA enforcement action is
  currently active.
- Deferring Article 6 admissibility capability because no Article 6 transfer
  has yet been executed.
- Treating EU CBAM admissibility as optional because no regulated goods have
  yet been exported.
- Treating Gold Standard as a lower priority than Verra because fewer Symphony
  projects currently use the Gold Standard methodology.

All regimes are constitutionally active from the moment Symphony operates within
their jurisdiction domain, regardless of the current state of enforcement activity.
