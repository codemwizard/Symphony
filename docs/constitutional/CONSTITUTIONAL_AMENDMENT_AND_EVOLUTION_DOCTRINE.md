## Purpose

This document defines the constitutional legality of modifying Symphony doctrine itself. It establishes the procedures, constraints, authority requirements, and prohibition boundaries that govern all amendments to Symphony's constitutional substrate, including ontology mutation, sovereignty-boundary modification, replay-obligation modification, admissibility continuity preservation, regulator-boundary evolution, and phase-doctrine evolution.

No constitutional amendment, ontology change, sovereignty redefinition, or doctrine supersession is legally valid unless it complies in full with the procedures, constraints, and admissibility requirements defined herein.

---

## Constitutional Scope

This doctrine governs:

1. All modifications to Symphony constitutional documents, including root doctrine, phase doctrine, wave doctrine, enforcement doctrine, and regulator-partition doctrine.
2. All ontology mutations, including additions, deletions, reclassifications, and semantic redefinitions of constitutional concepts.
3. All modifications to sovereignty-boundary definitions, authority-rank assignments, replay obligations, admissibility continuity requirements, and regulator-coexistence constraints.
4. All supersession events, including partial supersession, scoped supersession, and full replacement.
5. All constitutional compatibility assertions made in support of doctrine evolution.
6. All historical constitutional reconstruction obligations.

This doctrine does NOT govern:

- Runtime operational decisions within constitutionally bounded phases.
- Migration tip evolution within wave-layer authority structures, except where migration content constitutes de facto constitutional amendment.
- Individual enforcement trigger parameter adjustments that do not alter constitutional semantics.

## Authority Boundaries

### Amendment Authority Requirements

A constitutional amendment is legally valid only when ALL of the following authority conditions are satisfied:

**A1. Root Authority Authorship**
The amendment must originate from a Root-authority document or a designated Constitutional Custodian operating under Root authority delegation. No lower-layer authority (Regulatory, Phase, Enforcement) may originate an amendment to a higher-layer constitutional document.

**A2. Explicit Supersession Declaration**
The amendment must contain an explicit supersession declaration identifying, by canonical document name and version lineage reference, each prior constitutional document or section it supersedes, modifies, or extends.

**A3. Sovereignty Boundary Attestation**
The amendment must contain a Sovereignty Boundary Attestation confirming that the sovereignty domains governed by the amended document remain constitutionally distinct and have not been collapsed, merged, subordinated, or implicitly redefined.

**A4. Replay Obligation Continuity Attestation**
The amendment must contain a Replay Obligation Continuity Attestation confirming that all existing replay obligations are preserved, extended if required, or explicitly transitioned with a replay-safe migration path. No amendment may reduce, eliminate, or render ambiguous any replay obligation without a superseding replay obligation of equal or greater constitutional weight.

**A5. Admissibility Continuity Attestation**
The amendment must contain an Admissibility Continuity Attestation confirming that all evidence, events, and records admitted under prior constitutional doctrine remain admissible under the amended doctrine, or are explicitly transitioned to an admissibility-preserving classification.

**A6. Regulator-Partition Consistency Attestation**
The amendment must contain a Regulator-Partition Consistency Attestation confirming that the amendment does not redefine, merge, subordinate, or implicitly collapse any regulator sovereignty domain.

**A7. Phase-Doctrine Compatibility Declaration**
The amendment must contain a Phase-Doctrine Compatibility Declaration confirming that the amendment is consistent with the constitutional capability boundaries of all active and deferred phases.

**A8. Doctrine Version Lineage Registration**
The amendment must register a new doctrine version in the constitutional history record, linking to all prior versions in the supersession chain. The prior version must remain reconstructable in full.

## Ontology Mutation Legality

### Permitted Ontology Mutations

The following ontology mutations are constitutionally permitted when accompanied by full amendment authority attestations:

- **Additive ontology extension:** Introduction of new constitutional concepts, roles, authorities, or domains that do not redefine or subordinate existing concepts.
- **Clarifying refinement:** Disambiguation of an existing concept's scope where the refinement is strictly narrowing and does not alter the concept's authority rank or sovereignty domain.
- **Phased ontology deferral:** Classification of a concept as deferred within a specified phase, provided the deferred status is explicitly bounded and does not constitute de facto elimination.
- **Supersession with continuity:** Replacement of an existing concept with a successor concept, provided the successor preserves all prior replay obligations, admissibility continuity, and sovereignty boundaries.

### Prohibited Ontology Mutations

The following ontology mutations are constitutionally prohibited:

- **Silent concept elimination:** Removal of a constitutional concept without explicit supersession declaration and historical lineage preservation.
- **Authority rank reduction without supersession:** Reclassification of an existing concept to a lower authority rank without a formally documented supersession event.
- **Semantic collapse of distinct domains:** Redefinition of two or more constitutionally distinct concepts as equivalent, interchangeable, or subordinate without explicit constitutional authority.
- **Indirect mutation through lower-layer documents:** Introduction of ontology changes via enforcement documents, migration content, or phase documents that effectively redefine root constitutional concepts without root-authority amendment.
- **Replay-destructive ontology mutation:** Any ontology change that renders prior replay events inadmissible, unresolvable, or unverifiable.

## Sovereignty-Boundary Modification Legality

### General Principle

Sovereignty boundaries in Symphony are constitutionally orthogonal by design. Wave 4 (operational/runtime sovereignty) and Wave 8 (provenance/cryptographic sovereignty) constitute independent and non-subordinate sovereignty domains. Regulator domains are constitutionally partitioned and may not be collapsed under any amendment procedure.

### Permitted Sovereignty-Boundary Modifications

- **Boundary clarification:** Explicit narrowing or disambiguation of a sovereignty domain's scope, provided no existing sovereignty surface is absorbed into another domain.
- **New domain introduction:** Introduction of a new sovereignty domain, provided it does not overlap with, subordinate, or implicitly absorb an existing domain.
- **Phase-scoped boundary extension:** Extension of a sovereignty boundary's application to a newly introduced phase, provided the extension does not alter the boundary's authority within existing phases.

### Prohibited Sovereignty-Boundary Modifications

- **Domain merger:** Any modification that causes two constitutionally distinct sovereignty domains to be treated as a single domain, whether explicitly or implicitly.
- **Runtime supremacy assertion:** Any modification asserting that the operational/runtime sovereignty layer (Wave 4) supersedes, overrides, or subsumes the provenance/cryptographic sovereignty layer (Wave 8), or vice versa.
- **Regulator equivalence assertion:** Any modification treating distinct regulator domains as equivalent, interchangeable, or mutually subordinate.
- **Convergence-by-silence:** Any modification that implicitly collapses sovereignty domains by omitting explicit boundary definitions.

## Replay-Obligation Modification Constraints

### Constitutional Status of Replay Obligations

Replay obligations in Symphony are constitutional permanence infrastructure. They are not implementation artifacts. Replay obligations define the constitutional requirement that the evidentiary state of the platform at any prior constitutional moment must be reconstructable from the historical record.

### Permitted Replay-Obligation Modifications

- **Extension of replay scope:** Expansion of replay obligations to cover additional record classes, event types, or historical periods.
- **Replay mechanism clarification:** Disambiguation of the technical mechanism by which replay obligations are satisfied, provided the underlying obligation is preserved.
- **Replay-safe phase transitions:** Definition of replay obligations for newly introduced phases, with explicit linkage to prior phase replay obligations.

### Prohibited Replay-Obligation Modifications

- **Replay obligation reduction:** Any modification that reduces the scope, depth, or enforceability of any existing replay obligation.
- **Replay-destructive constitutional evolution:** Any constitutional amendment that renders previously replayable events unreplayable, including through ontology mutation, admissibility reclassification, or sovereignty-boundary redefinition.
- **Silent replay elimination:** Removal of a replay obligation without explicit supersession by an equal or greater obligation.
- **Retroactive replay invalidation:** Any amendment asserting that prior replay events are no longer constitutionally valid, admissible, or reconstructable.

## Admissibility Continuity Preservation

### General Principle

Historical admissibility continuity is an unconditional constitutional requirement. Records, evidence, events, and transactions admitted under any prior constitutional doctrine retain their admissibility status under all subsequent constitutional states. No constitutional amendment may retroactively invalidate the admissibility of any previously admitted record.

### Admissibility Continuity Obligations

Every constitutional amendment must:

1. Explicitly confirm that all records admitted under prior doctrine remain admissible.
2. Identify any records whose admissibility classification is being extended, narrowed, or transitioned, with explicit statement of the transition path.
3. Preserve the historical constitutional state sufficient to reconstruct the admissibility basis for any prior-admitted record at the time of its original admission.

### Prohibited Admissibility Modifications

- **Retroactive admissibility invalidation:** Any amendment declaring previously admitted records inadmissible.
- **Admissibility basis erasure:** Any amendment that removes or obscures the constitutional basis upon which prior records were admitted, rendering their admissibility unreconstructable.
- **Silent admissibility reclassification:** Any reclassification of an admissibility category that has the effect of excluding previously admitted records without explicit transition doctrine.

## Regulator-Boundary Evolution Constraints

### Constitutional Principle of Regulator Orthogonality

Regulator domains in Symphony are constitutionally partitioned. Each regulator domain constitutes a distinct and independent sovereign context with its own admissibility standards, evidence requirements, and enforcement surfaces. No regulator domain is constitutionally superior, inferior, or equivalent to any other.

### Permitted Regulator-Boundary Evolution

- **Introduction of new regulator domain:** Addition of a new regulator domain with explicitly defined boundaries, provided no existing regulator domain is absorbed, merged, or implicitly redefined.
- **Regulator-domain scope clarification:** Explicit disambiguation of a regulator domain's constitutional scope, provided no other domain's boundaries are altered.

### Prohibited Regulator-Boundary Modifications

- **Regulator domain merger:** Any modification treating two or more regulator domains as a single domain.
- **Regulator hierarchy assertion:** Any modification asserting that one regulator domain's standards, requirements, or determinations supersede those of another.
- **Cross-regulator admissibility collapse:** Any modification asserting that admissibility under one regulator domain constitutes admissibility under another.
- **Regulator equivalence by omission:** Any amendment that omits explicit regulator-boundary definitions, permitting implied equivalence.

## Phase-Doctrine Evolution Legality

### Constitutional Principle of Phase Capability Boundaries

Phases in Symphony constitute constitutional capability boundaries. Each phase defines the set of operations, records, and authorities that are constitutionally legal within that phase. A record produced outside the constitutional capability boundary of its producing phase is constitutionally inadmissible.

### Permitted Phase-Doctrine Evolution

- **Introduction of new phase:** Addition of a new constitutional phase with explicitly defined capability boundaries, entry conditions, and exit conditions.
- **Phase capability extension:** Expansion of a phase's constitutional capabilities, provided the extension does not retroactively render prior in-phase records inadmissible.
- **Phase boundary clarification:** Disambiguation of a phase's capability boundary, provided no existing in-phase records are retroactively excluded.

### Prohibited Phase-Doctrine Modifications

- **Retroactive phase reclassification:** Any modification reclassifying records produced under one phase as having been produced under a different phase.
- **Phase capability reduction with retroactive effect:** Any reduction of a phase's constitutional capabilities that renders previously admitted in-phase records inadmissible.
- **Phase-illegality misreading:** Any interpretation asserting that a phase's capability boundary exclusion applies retroactively to records produced before the boundary definition was established.
- **Silent phase elimination:** Removal of a constitutional phase without explicit supersession doctrine preserving the admissibility of all records produced in that phase.

## Supersession Legality

### Supersession Requirements

A supersession event is constitutionally valid only when:

1. The superseding document explicitly names the superseded document or section by canonical identifier.
2. The superseding document provides a complete doctrine version lineage entry linking the new version to all prior versions.
3. The superseded document or section remains reconstructable in full from the historical constitutional record.
4. The supersession does not reduce replay obligations, eliminate admissibility continuity, collapse sovereignty domains, or merge regulator partitions.
5. The supersession event is registered in the constitutional history record prior to any operational reliance on the superseding doctrine.

### Partial Supersession

A constitutional document may be partially superseded, provided:

- The partially superseded sections are explicitly identified by section identifier.
- The non-superseded sections of the prior document retain full constitutional authority.
- The partial supersession does not produce internal constitutional contradiction within the prior document's remaining operative sections.

### Supersession Cannot Erase Historical Constitutional State

Supersession is a forward-looking constitutional act. It transitions authority from one constitutional state to the next. It does not and cannot retroactively alter, erase, or invalidate the historical constitutional state that existed under the superseded doctrine. The prior constitutional state must remain reconstructable for all replay, admissibility, and evidentiary purposes.

## Doctrine Version Lineage

### Version Lineage Requirements

Every constitutional document subject to amendment must maintain a doctrine version lineage record that includes:

- **Document canonical identifier:** The stable, unique identifier for the constitutional document.
- **Version sequence:** An ordered sequence of all versions, from initial adoption to current.
- **Supersession chain:** For each version, the identifier of the version it supersedes and the version that supersedes it (if any).
- **Amendment authority record:** For each version transition, the authority class that authorized the amendment.
- **Effective date:** The constitutional moment at which each version became authoritative.
- **Reconstruction reference:** The location in the historical constitutional record from which the full text of each prior version can be reconstructed.

### Historical Constitutional Reconstruction Obligations

The constitutional history of Symphony must remain reconstructable in full at any point in time. This obligation requires:

1. No version of any constitutional document may be deleted from the historical record.
2. The supersession chain for every constitutional document must be resolvable without ambiguity.
3. The constitutional state of Symphony at any prior constitutional moment must be determinable by traversal of the version lineage records.
4. Replay events must be evaluable against the constitutional state that was authoritative at the time the replayed event occurred, not the current constitutional state.

## Prohibited Amendment Patterns

### Pattern 1: Silent Constitutional Drift

**Description:** Accumulated minor modifications to enforcement documents, migration content, or phase specifications that collectively alter constitutional semantics without a formal amendment event.

**Why Prohibited:** Silent drift constitutes indirect ontology mutation through lower-layer documents. It bypasses amendment authority requirements and renders the constitutional history non-reconstructable.

**Detection Signal:** When the operational behavior of any enforcement surface diverges from the constitutional semantics described in the authoritative root doctrine without a traceable supersession event.

### Pattern 2: Replay-Destructive Evolution

**Description:** A constitutional amendment that reclassifies a previously replayable event class as non-replayable, whether through ontology mutation, admissibility reclassification, or sovereignty-boundary redefinition.

**Why Prohibited:** Replay obligations are constitutional permanence infrastructure. Their reduction destroys the evidentiary integrity of the platform's historical record and constitutes a constitutional violation regardless of the authority level of the amending document.

**Detection Signal:** When an amendment includes language such as "prior events of class X are no longer subject to replay obligations" or "replay obligations for phase N are superseded without replacement."

### Pattern 3: Sovereignty-Collapse Amendment

**Description:** An amendment asserting or implying that Wave 4 (operational sovereignty) and Wave 8 (provenance/cryptographic sovereignty) constitute a single unified sovereignty layer.

**Why Prohibited:** The orthogonality of Wave 4 and Wave 8 is a foundational constitutional design principle. Their collapse would eliminate the constitutional distinction between runtime operational authority and cryptographic provenance authority, rendering the trust arbitration architecture non-functional.

**Detection Signal:** Language such as "Wave 4 and Wave 8 authority surfaces are unified under runtime governance" or "cryptographic provenance is subordinate to operational consensus."

### Pattern 4: Retroactive Admissibility Invalidation

**Description:** An amendment asserting that records admitted under prior constitutional doctrine are no longer admissible because the constitutional basis for their admission has been superseded.

**Why Prohibited:** Historical admissibility continuity is unconditional. Supersession of a constitutional doctrine transitions authority forward; it does not retroactively invalidate records admitted under the prior doctrine.

**Detection Signal:** Language such as "records admitted under the superseded doctrine are no longer admissible" or "admissibility of pre-amendment records must be re-evaluated under current doctrine."

### Pattern 5: Undocumented Constitutional Reinterpretation

**Description:** Reinterpretation of constitutional doctrine through operational guidance documents, agent instructions, or implementation specifications that do not constitute formal constitutional amendments.

**Why Prohibited:** Constitutional reinterpretation is a constitutional amendment event and must satisfy all amendment authority requirements. Reinterpretation through lower-authority documents constitutes an authority-rank violation.

**Detection Signal:** Operational or implementation documents that contain language beginning with "for purposes of this implementation, [constitutional concept] means..." where the stated meaning diverges from the authoritative constitutional definition.

2. The amendment confirms that all events previously attributed to Wave 4 authority retain their Wave 4 classification.
3. The amendment confirms that all events previously attributed to Wave 8 authority retain their Wave 8 classification.
4. No prior event's replay classification is altered.
5. The amendment is registered in the constitutional history record with full supersession chain linkage.

## Constitutional Compatibility Requirements

A constitutional amendment is constitutionally compatible with the existing doctrine corpus if and only if:

1. **Non-contradiction:** The amendment does not assert any proposition that directly contradicts an operative proposition in any non-superseded constitutional document of equal or higher authority rank.
2. **Sovereignty coherence:** The amendment does not introduce a sovereignty domain definition that overlaps with, subordinates, or implicitly merges any existing sovereignty domain.
3. **Replay consistency:** The amendment does not reduce the replay obligation coverage established by any prior constitutional document.
4. **Admissibility monotonicity:** The amendment does not reduce the set of admissible record classes established by prior constitutional doctrine.
5. **Phase legality preservation:** The amendment does not reclassify any in-phase record as constitutionally inadmissible.
6. **Regulator partition integrity:** The amendment does not alter the constitutional independence of any regulator domain.

## Prohibited Misinterpretations

**Invalid simplification — Amendment as version control:**
This doctrine must not be interpreted as a software version control policy. Constitutional amendments govern sovereign authority transitions, not code revisions. The constitutional history record is not a git log.

**Invalid simplification — Supersession as deletion:**
Supersession of a prior constitutional document does not delete, erase, or render inaccessible that document's historical constitutional state. The prior state remains reconstructable and replay-relevant in perpetuity.

**Forbidden authority collapse — Lower-layer amendment authority:**
Phase documents, enforcement documents, migration content, and operational guidance documents do not possess constitutional amendment authority. Any modification to root constitutional doctrine made through such documents constitutes an illegal constitutional act regardless of operational intent.

**Replay-destructive interpretation — Forward supersession eliminates replay:**
The existence of a superseding doctrine does not eliminate the replay obligations of the superseded doctrine. Replay must be executable against the constitutional state that was authoritative at the time of the replayed event. Supersession is not retroactive.

**Regulator-flattening interpretation — Universal admissibility:**
Admissibility of a record under one regulator domain does not constitute admissibility under any other regulator domain. Regulator domains are constitutionally partitioned and their admissibility standards are not transferable.

**Phase-illegality misreading — Current phase retroactivity:**
The constitutional capability boundaries of the current phase do not apply retroactively to records produced in prior phases. Records are evaluated against the constitutional state of the phase in which they were produced.

**Provenance/runtime collapse — Wave unification:**
Wave 4 and Wave 8 are constitutionally orthogonal sovereignty domains. No amendment may assert their unification, equivalence, or mutual subordination. The parallel existence of Wave 4 and Wave 8 authority surfaces does not imply architectural redundancy, conflict, or consolidation obligation.

**Convergence misreading — Inactive substrate implies eliminability:**
The existence of inactive, dormant, or unwired constitutional substrate does not constitute grounds for constitutional elimination of that substrate. Dormant sovereignty surfaces retain their constitutional status and must be treated in accordance with their declared authority class.

**Drift legitimization — Accumulated lower-layer modifications:**
No accumulation of lower-layer modifications, regardless of volume or operational significance, constitutes a legitimate constitutional amendment. Silent constitutional drift is prohibited regardless of whether the drift produces operationally coherent behavior.

**Reinterpretation license — Implementation documents:**
Implementation specifications, agent instructions, operational runbooks, and migration documentation do not possess authority to reinterpret constitutional doctrine. Any divergence between such documents and this doctrine must be resolved by formal constitutional amendment, not by operational reinterpretation.
