- **Analytical conclusion:** No analysis, synthesis, or inference — however sophisticated, however accurate — that concludes a historical record is no longer necessary constitutes grounds for that record's alteration or deletion.

### 1.3 The Priority Declaration

Replay survivability is Priority 1 in Symphony's constitutional priority ordering, as established in CONSTITUTIONAL_PRIORITY_AND_CONFLICT_ARBITRATION.md. This means:

- It yields to no other constitutional obligation in any conflict.

## Part II: Historical Truth Continuity Supremacy

### 2.1 Definition

Historical truth continuity supremacy is the constitutional principle that the evidentiary truth of Symphony's constitutional record — what occurred, who authorized it, when it was recorded, what its provenance chain establishes — is continuous across all time and all constitutional evolution. The truth of what was recorded does not change because the constitutional architecture that surrounded the recording has evolved.

### 2.2 Historical Truth Continuity Obligations

**HTC-1. The record speaks for itself at its constitutional moment.**
A record's constitutional truth is established at the moment of its production and admission. The record speaks for itself at that constitutional moment. Subsequent events — architectural evolution, policy change, key rotation, phase transition, regulatory change — do not alter what the record said and what it established at its constitutional moment.

**HTC-2. Historical truth is not subject to revision.**
No constitutional actor, no constitutional document, and no constitutional amendment may revise the historical truth of a record in the evidentiary chain. An amendment may change future constitutional requirements; it cannot change what prior records established. Historical truth is not amendable.

**HTC-3. Contradictory present-time assessments do not override historical truth.**
Where a present-time assessment — by an enforcement surface, by a regulator, by an analytical synthesis, by an external verifier — produces a conclusion that appears to contradict the historical truth of a prior record, the historical truth of the prior record is not altered. The present-time assessment may document a current condition; it may not revise a historical one.

**HTC-4. The completeness obligation.**
Historical truth continuity requires completeness: no gap in the constitutional record. Every record that was constitutionally admitted must remain in the constitutional record. Every signing event must have a corresponding entry in the signing audit trail. Every migration must be retained in sequence. Every constitutional doctrine version must be retained in the version lineage. A constitutional record with gaps is a constitutional record with broken truth continuity.

**HTC-5. The non-falsifiability obligation.**
No record in Symphony's constitutional evidentiary chain may be falsified — altered, replaced, or supplemented in a manner that changes its constitutional meaning — without creating a separately documented constitutional amendment event. Silent alteration of any record in the evidentiary chain constitutes a constitutional fraud, regardless of the intent of the altering actor.

## Part III: Provenance Permanence Doctrine

### 3.1 Definition

Provenance permanence doctrine is the constitutional principle that every cryptographic attestation — every Ed25519 signature, every signing hash, every HSM attestation record, every signer authorization entry — that was constitutionally valid at the time of its creation remains a constitutionally permanent evidentiary record of that validity. Provenance events are not transient. They are constitutional facts.

### 3.2 Provenance Permanence Rules

**PP-1. Signing events are permanent constitutional records.**
Every signing event — the application of an Ed25519 signature to a canonicalized record by an authorized signer — is a permanent constitutional record. It establishes that at a specific constitutional moment, a specific authorized signer attested to the canonical form of a specific record. This fact is permanent.

**PP-2. Signing event permanence is not affected by subsequent key lifecycle events.**
The permanence of a signing event is not conditional on the continued authorization of the signing key. A signing event that occurred while the signing key was authorized is permanently valid as a record of that authorized signing event, regardless of subsequent key rotation, key retirement, or signer revocation. The event's constitutional status at its production time is permanent; subsequent key lifecycle events are prospective.

**PP-3. Failed provenance events are also permanent.**
A Wave 8 FAIL determination — a record's failure to satisfy cryptographic provenance requirements — is itself a permanent constitutional record. The constitutional fact that a specific record failed Wave 8 provenance verification at a specific constitutional moment is as permanent as the constitutional fact that a different record passed. Both facts are part of Symphony's constitutional truth.

**PP-4. Shadow authority surface events are permanent constitutional defects.**
Records admitted through the `verify_ed25519_signature` stub's shadow authority surface — admitted as PASS/STUB — carry a permanent constitutional record of their Wave 8 provenance defect. This defect is a permanent constitutional fact. It is not erased by subsequent remediation, by the passage of time, or by present-time enforcement of actual cryptographic verification. The defect is documented; remediation (if executed through a constitutional resign operation) creates a new constitutional fact from the resign event forward; it does not erase the original defect.

**PP-5. The provenance chain is an unbroken temporal record.**
Symphony's Wave 8 hash chain constitutes an unbroken temporal record of provenance attestation events. Each link in the chain establishes that the prior link was known at the time of the subsequent link's creation. The chain's continuity is a constitutional record of continuous provenance tracking. Any break in the chain is a constitutional fact — a provenance gap — that must be documented, not concealed.

A record that was constitutionally admitted — that satisfied the applicable Wave 4 and Wave 8 requirements at the time of its production — is permanently replay-admissible. Its replay admissibility does not expire. It does not diminish over time. It does not require renewal.

## Part V: Historical Verification Non-Destruction

### 5.1 Definition

Historical verification non-destruction is the constitutional obligation that no constitutional actor, no constitutional amendment, and no operational event may destroy, degrade, or render inaccessible the evidentiary infrastructure necessary to verify the historical constitutional validity of any admitted record.

Historical verification infrastructure includes: the records themselves, their signing attestations, the historical signer registry, the historical canonicalization registry, the hash chain context, the trust-root lineage record, and the constitutional doctrine version lineage.

### 5.2 Non-Destruction Obligations

**HVN-1. Records may not be deleted.**
No admitted record may be deleted from Symphony's constitutional evidentiary store. The constitutional evidentiary store is append-only in its admitted record content. Deletion of an admitted record destroys the evidentiary basis for replay at that record's position in the hash chain and for all subsequent records whose hash chain anchor depends on the deleted record.

**HVN-2. Signing attestations may not be deleted.**
No signing attestation — including `signing_audit_log` entries, HSM attestation records, and signature fields within records — may be deleted. Signing attestations are the Wave 8 evidentiary basis for replay admissibility.

**HVN-3. Signer registry historical entries may not be deleted.**
No historical entry in `wave8_signer_resolution` or `delegated_signing_grants` — including entries for retired, rotated, or revoked signers and keys — may be deleted. These entries constitute the evidentiary basis for establishing signer authorization at prior constitutional moments. Their deletion destroys the replay verifiability of records signed during the deleted entry's active period.

**HVN-4. Canonicalization schema versions may not be deleted.**
No canonicalization schema version registered in `canonicalization_registry` may be deleted. Each schema version is the evidentiary basis for reconstructing the canonical form of records signed under that version. Their deletion renders the signature of every record signed under the deleted version unverifiable.

**HVN-5. Migration records may not be deleted or reordered.**
No migration record in the constitutional migration sequence may be deleted or reordered. The migration sequence is the constitutional evidentiary record of Wave 4 enforcement surface evolution. Deletion or reordering destroys temporal proof-of-state for all constitutional moments that depended on the deleted or reordered migration.

**HVN-6. Constitutional doctrine versions may not be erased from the version lineage.**
No constitutional doctrine version may be erased from the doctrine version lineage record. The version lineage is the constitutional evidentiary record of which doctrine was authoritative at any prior constitutional moment. Its completeness is required for replay-version governance (per TEMPORAL_VALIDITY_AND_REPLAY_DOCTRINE.md, Part VI).

**HVN-7. Trust-root lineage entries may not be deleted.**
No trust-root lineage entry may be deleted. The trust-root lineage record is the constitutional evidentiary basis for determining which trust root governed any prior constitutional moment. Its completeness is required for all replay events involving records produced under prior trust roots.

**HVN-8. Phase transition records may not be deleted.**
No phase transition record may be deleted. Phase transition records are the constitutional evidentiary basis for determining which phase was active at any prior constitutional moment, and therefore which phase capability boundaries governed records produced at that time.

## Part VIII: Admissibility Continuity Obligations

### 8.1 Definition

Admissibility continuity obligations are the constitutional requirements that the admissibility status of every historically admitted record is preserved across all constitutional evolution. Every record that was constitutionally admitted at the time of its production retains that admissibility permanently. No subsequent constitutional evolution disrupts the admissibility continuum.

### 8.2 Admissibility Continuity Rules

**AC-1. Admissibility is established once and is permanent.**
A record's admissibility status is established at the moment of its constitutional admission. Once established, that status is permanent for the historical constitutional moment of its production. It is not subject to re-evaluation, re-determination, or revocation by subsequent constitutional evolution.

**AC-2. Admissibility continuity is not conditional on constitutional stability.**
Admissibility continuity applies regardless of how significantly Symphony's constitutional architecture evolves after a record's production. A record admitted in Phase 0 under the constitutional doctrine and enforcement surfaces operative at that time retains its Phase 0 admissibility status unconditionally through all subsequent phases and all subsequent constitutional amendments.

**AC-3. Admissibility continuity across regulator domain evolution.**
The historical admissibility of a record within a regulator domain is preserved across that domain's subsequent evolution. If Domain A subsequently introduces more stringent admissibility requirements, records admitted under prior Domain A requirements retain their historical Domain A admissibility.

**AC-4. Admissibility continuity across wave doctrine evolution.**
The historical admissibility of a record within Wave 4 operational sovereignty is preserved across subsequent Wave 4 doctrine evolution. The historical admissibility of a record within Wave 8 provenance sovereignty is preserved across subsequent Wave 8 doctrine evolution.

**AC-5. New admissibility standards are prospective.**
All new admissibility standards — whether introduced by constitutional amendment, by new enforcement surfaces, or by regulator domain requirement evolution — apply prospectively to records produced after their constitutional effective date. They do not re-evaluate or alter the admissibility of prior records.

**AC-6. Admissibility continuity obligations are symmetric.**
The admissibility continuity obligation protects both directions of the admissibility spectrum. It protects historically admitted records from retroactive inadmissibility (prohibition of retroactive invalidation). It also protects historically inadmissible records from retroactive admission by relaxed present-time standards (per TEMPORAL_VALIDITY_AND_REPLAY_DOCTRINE.md, PTE-4). Historical constitutional facts are preserved in both directions.

## Part IX: Replay-Safe Evolution Constraints

### 9.1 Definition

Replay-safe evolution constraints are the constitutional requirements that all evolution of Symphony's architecture — all amendments, all migrations, all enforcement surface changes, all key lifecycle events, all schema changes — be executed in a manner that preserves full replay survivability for all historically admitted records.

### 9.2 Replay-Safe Evolution Test

Every proposed constitutional evolution must satisfy the following replay-safe evolution test before it may be constitutionally authorized:

**RSE-T1. Historical record integrity:** Does the proposed evolution alter, delete, or render inaccessible any record in the constitutional evidentiary store? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T2. Signing attestation integrity:** Does the proposed evolution alter, delete, or render inaccessible any signing attestation? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T3. Signer registry historical integrity:** Does the proposed evolution delete any historical entry from `wave8_signer_resolution`, `delegated_signing_grants`, or `public_keys_registry`? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T4. Canonicalization registry integrity:** Does the proposed evolution delete any entry from `canonicalization_registry`? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T5. Migration sequence integrity:** Does the proposed evolution delete, reorder, or retroactively amend any migration in the constitutional migration sequence? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T6. Doctrine version lineage integrity:** Does the proposed evolution erase any version from the constitutional doctrine version lineage? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T7. Trust-root lineage integrity:** Does the proposed evolution delete any entry from the trust-root lineage record? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T8. External verifier independence:** After the proposed evolution, does an external verifier retain the ability to reconstruct and verify the constitutional validity of all historically admitted records without access to Symphony's runtime infrastructure? If no, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible.

**RSE-T9. Temporal continuity:** Does the proposed evolution create any gap in the constitutional record's temporal continuity? If yes, the evolution is replay-unsafe and constitutionally impermissible unless the gap is a constitutionally documented event (e.g., a trust root compromise period) that has already been recorded as a constitutional fact.

A proposed evolution that fails any single test element is constitutionally impermissible. The test is conjunctive: all nine elements must be satisfied.

### 9.3 Replay-Safe Evolution Examples

**RSE-Example-1: Key rotation.**
A signing key rotation retires Key A and activates Key B in `wave8_signer_resolution`. Replay-safe evolution test: RSE-T3 — the Key A entry is not deleted; it is marked as retired with a retirement timestamp. PASS. All nine test elements satisfied. The evolution is replay-safe.

**RSE-Example-2: Schema supersession.**
A new canonicalization schema version V3 is registered in `canonicalization_registry`. Schema version V2 remains in the registry as a retired version. Replay-safe evolution test: RSE-T4 — V2 is not deleted. PASS. All nine test elements satisfied. The evolution is replay-safe.

**RSE-Example-3: Phase transition.**
Constitutional Phase 2 is entered and Phase 1 is retired. Phase 1's phase transition record (with its transition timestamp) is added to the constitutional record. Replay-safe evolution test: RSE-T1 through RSE-T9 — no historical records deleted, no registries purged, no temporal gap created. PASS. The evolution is replay-safe.

**RSE-Example-4: Wave 8 enforcement activation.**
`wave8_cryptographic_enforcement` is activated on the `state_transitions` write path via a new migration. Prior records (admitted under the pre-activation enforcement state) are not retroactively subjected to the new enforcement. Replay-safe evolution test: RSE-T1 — prior records not altered. PASS. The evolution is replay-safe.

## Part X: Prohibited Replay-Destructive Optimizations

The following optimization patterns are constitutionally prohibited. Their application to Symphony's constitutional evidentiary infrastructure constitutes a replay survivability violation — a constitutional defect of the highest order, directly contradicting the supreme constitutional priority.

### 10.1 Storage Compression of Admitted Records

**Prohibited pattern:** Applying lossy or destructive compression to admitted records, their provenance fields, or their signing attestations to reduce storage consumption.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates HVN-1, HVN-2, RSE-T1, RSE-T2. Compression that reduces the evidentiary fidelity of a record constitutes alteration of the record's constitutional content. Even lossless compression that requires decompression infrastructure introduces an external dependency that may compromise the external verifier independence obligation (EVI-3 in CRYPTOGRAPHIC_AND_RUNTIME_AUTHORITY_DOCTRINE.md).

**Permissible alternative:** Lossless retention of full-fidelity records. Storage cost is a constitutionally non-governing factor. Optimization of non-replay-obligated operational data does not implicate this prohibition.

### 10.2 Tiered Storage with Degraded Accessibility

**Prohibited pattern:** Moving replay-obligated records to tiered storage systems with retrieval latencies, access restrictions, or format changes that would impair an external verifier's ability to access and verify them within a constitutionally acceptable timeframe.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates RSE-T8. If an external verifier cannot access replay-obligated records without special infrastructure, credentials, or extended delays, external verifier independence is compromised.

**Permissible alternative:** Tiered storage that maintains full external accessibility — where an external verifier with appropriate constitutional authorization can retrieve and verify any record without runtime infrastructure dependency.

### 10.3 Signer Registry Purging

**Prohibited pattern:** Deleting, archiving, or otherwise removing historical entries from `wave8_signer_resolution`, `delegated_signing_grants`, or `public_keys_registry` on the basis that the signers are no longer active, the keys are no longer authorized, or the delegations have expired.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates HVN-3, RSE-T3. Historical signer registry entries are the evidentiary basis for establishing signer authorization at prior constitutional moments. Their removal destroys the replay verifiability of all records signed by the removed signers during their authorized period.

**Permissible alternative:** Status updates (marking entries as retired, revoked, or expired with precise timestamps) that preserve the historical entry while documenting its current inactive status.

### 10.4 Canonicalization Schema Deprecation with Deletion

**Prohibited pattern:** Removing prior canonicalization schema versions from `canonicalization_registry` on the basis that they are no longer used for new records.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates HVN-4, RSE-T4. Every existing signed record whose signing hash was produced under a deleted schema version becomes cryptographically unverifiable upon the schema's deletion. The deletion simultaneously invalidates the replay admissibility of all such records.

**Permissible alternative:** Status updates marking schema versions as retired with retirement timestamps, while retaining the complete schema definition permanently.

### 10.5 Migration Sequence Compaction

**Prohibited pattern:** Collapsing, squashing, or compacting the constitutional migration sequence — merging multiple migrations into a single migration, removing intermediate migrations, or replacing the sequence with a single "current state" migration.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates HVN-5, RSE-T5. The migration sequence is the constitutional temporal record of Wave 4 enforcement surface evolution. Compaction destroys the ability to establish temporal proof-of-state at intermediate constitutional moments — the enforcement surface operative at any point between two retained migrations cannot be established from a compacted sequence.

**Permissible alternative:** Retention of the complete migration sequence. Operational efficiency concerns do not override the constitutional integrity of the migration record.

### 10.6 Log Rotation of Signing Audit Records

**Prohibited pattern:** Applying time-based log rotation policies to `signing_audit_log` or analogous signing audit infrastructure, purging entries older than a specified retention window.

**Constitutional prohibition basis:** Violates HVN-2, RSE-T2. Signing audit records constitute the non-repudiation infrastructure of Wave 8 provenance sovereignty. Their deletion destroys the constitutional auditability of the Wave 8 signing chain for the periods covered by the deleted entries.

**Permissible alternative:** No time-based purging of signing audit records. The signing audit trail is replay-obligated infrastructure subject to permanent retention.

### 10.7 Evidence Pack Field Reduction

**Prohibited pattern:** Modifying the schema of `evidence_packs` or the content of existing evidence pack records to reduce field density, eliminate fields deemed operationally unnecessary, or replace evidence content with references.
